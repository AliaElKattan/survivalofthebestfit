Survival of the Best Fit

The Game

Survival of the Best Fit is an educational game to teach the public about how blind usage of AI in hiring can inherit human bias and reinforce workforce inequality. In the game, users play the role of a startup CEO who's under a lot of pressure to grow the company fast. At first, they start by selecting the best candidates manually, but with overwhelming pressure and workload, resort to employing an AI system that they don't fully understand. The black box seems to be working at first, hiring much faster at a lower cost, but slowly the unforeseen consequences come bearing down.

Supported by the Creative Media Award from Mozilla Foundation. Built by Gabor, Jihyun, Miha, and Alia.

Because AI is not neutral

We as technologists have the responsibility to be well-aware of the potential social and ethical implications of the code we write. Much of the public debate on the development of AI has presented it as a threat imposed on us, rather than one that we have agency over. We want to change this by helping users understand the technology and demand more transparency and accountability from those building increasingly pervasive machine learning systems today. We want to break down the barrier between the average user and technologies they may deem too complicated to question.
